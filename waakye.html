Waakye with Tomato Stew Recipe

Description

Waakye is a beloved Ghanaian street food and household favorite, known for its distinctive reddish-brown hue derived from sorghum leaves or baking soda. This dish combines rice and beans, cooked together to create a flavorful, protein-packed meal, often served with spicy shito sauce, fried plantain, and a rich tomato stew.

Ready in about an hour, this recipe brings the vibrant energy of Ghanaian street markets to your kitchen. It’s perfect for breakfast, lunch, or dinner, and ideal for sharing with friends and family!

Ingredients

  • 1 cup black-eyed peas, soaked overnight
  • 2 cups long-grain rice
  • 4 cups water
  • 1 teaspoon baking soda (or 2 sorghum leaves)
  • 1 medium onion, chopped
  • 3 medium tomatoes, blended
  • 1/4 cup vegetable oil
  • 1 teaspoon ginger, grated
  • 1 scotch bonnet pepper, whole or chopped
  • 1 cup smoked fish, flaked
  • Salt to taste
  • Shito sauce, for serving
  • Fried plantain, for serving (optional)

Steps

  1. Rinse soaked beans and boil in 4 cups water with baking soda (or sorghum leaves) for 30 minutes until halfway cooked.
  2. Add rice to the pot, stir, and add more water if needed. Cook on low heat for 20-25 minutes until rice and beans are soft. Season with salt.
  3. In a separate pot, heat vegetable oil. Sauté onions and ginger for 3 minutes.
  4. Add blended tomatoes and scotch bonnet pepper. Cook for 10 minutes until the stew thickens.
  5. Stir in smoked fish and simmer for 5 minutes. Adjust seasoning with salt.
  6. Serve waakye hot with tomato stew, shito sauce, and fried plantain on the side.
